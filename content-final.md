**Headline (H2):**

**Body copy:**
> Children rarely sit down and talk about what's troubling them — they show it, in how they play, draw, build, and pretend. 4. **Every child, whole.** Home, school, friendships, development — we look at the full picture, and the little person in the middle of it.

## Section 8 — FAQ

**Purpose:** Answer the questions every worried parent has before they'll fill in a form.

**Headline (H2):**
> Questions parents *always* ask

**Q&A (accordion):**

1. **How do I know if my child needs play therapy?**
   There's no test you need to pass to reach out. If something about your child's behaviour, mood, or reactions has been worrying you for more than a few weeks — or a teacher has raised a concern — that's reason enough for a conversation. The first consultation is exactly for figuring this out together.

2. **What actually happens in a session?**
   Your child and Vishva spend the session in the playroom — playing, drawing, building, telling stories. It looks simple from the outside, but the toys and art materials are chosen so children can express and work through feelings safely. There's no pressure to talk about anything.

3. **Can play therapy help if my child is on the autism spectrum, or has ADHD?**
   Yes — this is a core part of Vishva's training and daily work. Play and creative arts therapy doesn't demand sitting still or talking things through; it meets your child in whatever way they naturally connect, and builds from there. Sessions are adapted to your child's sensory needs, interests, and pace.

4. **Do I stay with my child during sessions?**
   For most children, sessions are one-to-one with Vishva — that privacy is part of what makes the playroom feel safe. You'll be nearby, and for younger or more anxious children we ease into this gently. You'll never be pushed faster than your child is ready for.

5. **How many sessions will my child need?**
   It genuinely varies — some children need a handful of sessions, others benefit from longer support. What we promise: you'll never be left guessing. We review progress with you regularly, and you'll always know why we're continuing or winding down.

6. **What ages do you work with?**
   Children and young people from 4 to 14 years old. If your child is just outside this range, get in touch anyway — we'll point you in the right direction.

7. **What should I tell my child before the first session?**
   Keep it simple and honest: "You're going to meet Vishva. She has a room full of toys and art things, and you can play however you like." No need to mention problems or fixing anything — the playroom does that work gently.
