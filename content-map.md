## Section 2 — Is This for Your Child?

**Purpose:** Meet the worried parent where they are. A scannable grid of common concerns, followed by a reassurance line. (Pattern: Center4PlayTherapy's symptom checklist + TheraPeace's "it often starts with a small worry" framing.)

**Headline (H2):**
> Does any of this feel familiar?

**Concern grid (6 cards, icon + label + one line each):**

1. **Big feelings & meltdowns** — Tantrums that feel bigger than the moment, or tears that come out of nowhere.
2. **Worry & anxiety** — Clinginess, tummy aches before school, fear of being away from you.
3. **Friendship struggles** — Trouble making friends, sharing, or joining in with other children.
4. **Changes at home** — A new sibling, a house move, separation, or a loss in the family.
5. **School concerns** — Refusing school, acting out in class, or a teacher who has raised a flag.
6. **Quiet withdrawal** — A child who has gone unusually silent, and you can't quite reach them.

**Reassurance line (below the grid):**
> If you nodded at any of these — none of it means something is "wrong" with your child. It usually means they're carrying something they don't yet have the words for. Play gives them another language.

## Section 3 — What Is Play Therapy? / Our Approach

**Purpose:** A short educational explainer (2–3 sentences, not the long-form block some references use) plus the practice's values — the "how we work" trust builder.

**Headline (H2):**
> Play is how children *speak*.

**Body copy:**
> Children rarely sit down and talk about what's troubling them — they show it, in how they play, draw, build, and pretend. Play therapy uses that natural language. In a room designed for them, with a therapist trained to listen through play, children express what they're carrying, make sense of it, and slowly set it down.

**Values row (4 points, short heading + one or two lines each):**

1. **Your child leads.** There's no "right way" to play here. Your child chooses, and that choice is where the healing starts.
2. **Safety comes first.** The playroom is a judgement-free space. What your child shares through play is treated with the same care and confidentiality as any therapy.
3. **Parents are partners.** You're never left outside the process — regular parent conversations are built into how we work.
4. **Every child, whole.** We look at the full picture: home, school, friendships, and the little person in the middle of it all.

**CTA:** none in this section.

## Section 8 — FAQ

**Purpose:** Answer the questions every worried parent has before they'll fill in a form. These seven questions recurred across the reference sites' FAQs (`structure-content.md`, Section C); answers drafted for The Healing Play.

**Headline (H2):**
> Questions parents *always* ask

**Q&A (accordion):**

1. **How do I know if my child needs play therapy?**
   There's no test you need to pass to reach out. If something about your child's behaviour, mood, or reactions has been worrying you for more than a few weeks — or a teacher has raised a concern — that's reason enough for a conversation. The first consultation is exactly for figuring this out together.

2. **What actually happens in a session?**
   Your child and Vishwa spend the session in the playroom — playing. It looks simple from the outside, but the toys, art materials, and games are chosen so children can express and work through feelings safely. There's no pressure to talk about anything.

3. **Do I stay with my child during sessions?**
   For most children, sessions are one-to-one with Vishwa — that privacy is part of what makes the playroom feel safe. You'll be nearby, and for very young or anxious children we ease into this gently. You'll never be pushed faster than your child is ready for.

4. **How many sessions will my child need?**
   It genuinely varies — some children need a handful of sessions, others benefit from longer support. What we promise: you'll never be left guessing. We review progress with you regularly and you'll always know why we're continuing or winding down.

5. **What ages do you work with?**
   `[CLIENT TO CONFIRM — e.g. "Children aged 3 to 12. For younger toddlers or teenagers, get in touch and we'll point you in the right direction."]`

6. **What should I tell my child before the first session?**
   Keep it simple and honest: "You're going to meet Vishwa. She has a room full of toys, and you can play however you like." No need to mention problems or fixing anything — the playroom does that work gently.

7. **Where are you located, and what are your timings?**
