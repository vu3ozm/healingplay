Does any of this feel familiar?

  • Big feelings & behaviour

    Meltdowns, anger, or reactions that feel bigger than the moment.

  • Worry & anxiety

    Clinginess, school-morning tummy aches, fears that won't settle.

  • Autism spectrum (ASD)

    Support for children on the spectrum, built around how your child connects and plays.

  • Attention & focus (ADHD)

    Restlessness, impulsivity, or focus struggles at home and school.

  • Social & school struggles

    Trouble making friends, joining in, or coping with school demands.

  • Hard life moments

    A loss, a move, family changes, or anything your child is quietly carrying.

If you nodded at any of these, none of it means something is “wrong” with your child. It means they're carrying something they don't yet have the words for. Play gives them another language.

Play is how children speak.

Children rarely sit down and talk about what's troubling them. They show it, in how they play, draw, build, and pretend. Play therapy uses that natural language: in a room designed for them, with a trained therapist alongside, children express what they're carrying, make sense of it, and slowly set it down.

At The Healing Play, sessions also draw on the creative arts, from art and music to storytelling, drama, and movement, so every child can find the medium that fits them. For children on the autism spectrum or with ADHD, this flexibility matters most of all: therapy adapts to your child, never the other way around.

  • Your child leads

    There's no “right way” to play here. Your child chooses, and that choice is where the healing starts.

  • Safety comes first

    The playroom is a judgement-free space, and what your child shares through play is treated with care and confidentiality.

  • Parents are partners

    Vishva works with parents and families, not just children. Regular parent conversations are built into the process.

  • Every child, whole

    Home, school, friendships, development: we look at the full picture, and the little person in the middle of it.

How we can help

Every child arrives with a different story, so every plan is built around your child, not a fixed program.

  • Child-centred play therapy

    One-to-one sessions for ages 4–14, where your child leads the play and healing follows at their pace.

  • Creative and expressive arts therapy

    Art, music, storytelling, and drama for children who express themselves better through making than talking.

  • Support for neurodivergent children

    Therapy adapted for children on the autism spectrum, with ADHD, or with special educational needs.

  • Parent and family counselling

    Sessions for you: understanding your child's inner world, and what actually helps at home.

  • Social-emotional learning groups

    Small-group sessions building self-awareness, friendship skills, and emotional vocabulary through games and art.

Questions parents always ask

How do I know if my child needs play therapy?

There's no test you need to pass to reach out. If something about your child's behaviour, mood, or reactions has been worrying you for more than a few weeks, or a teacher has raised a concern, that's reason enough for a conversation. The first consultation is exactly for figuring this out together.

What actually happens in a session?

Your child and Vishva spend the session in the playroom, playing, drawing, building, telling stories. It looks simple from the outside, but the toys and art materials are chosen so children can express and work through feelings safely. There's no pressure to talk about anything.

Can play therapy help if my child is on the autism spectrum, or has ADHD?

Yes, this is a core part of Vishva's training and daily work. Play and creative arts therapy doesn't demand sitting still or talking things through; it meets your child in whatever way they naturally connect, and builds from there. Sessions are adapted to your child's sensory needs, interests, and pace.

Do I stay with my child during sessions?

For most children, sessions are one-to-one with Vishva, and that privacy is part of what makes the playroom feel safe. You'll be nearby, and for younger or more anxious children we ease into this gently. You'll never be pushed faster than your child is ready for.

How many sessions will my child need?

It genuinely varies: some children need a handful of sessions, others benefit from longer support. What we promise: you'll never be left guessing. We review progress with you regularly, and you'll always know why we're continuing or winding down.

What ages do you work with?

Children and young people from 4 to 14 years old. If your child is just outside this range, get in touch anyway, and we'll point you in the right direction.

What should I tell my child before the first session?

Keep it simple and honest: “You're going to meet Vishva. She has a room full of toys and art things, and you can play however you like.” No need to mention problems or fixing anything. The playroom does that work gently.

What does it cost?

Fees are discussed in your first consultation, once we understand what your child needs. We'll always be clear and upfront, and there are no hidden charges.

Where are you located, and what are your timings?

We're based in Bodakdev, Ahmedabad, and you'll find directions in the contact section below. Sessions run Monday to Saturday, 11 am to 7 pm, with Sundays off.
