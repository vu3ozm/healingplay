type per section, and actual short-form content. Focus is **structure and content
only** — not visual style. The final section translates this into a single-page plan
for our client's site.

# Condensing to a Single Page — Analysis

Our client's site is a **single scrolling page**. Below: which multi-page content collapses
into one section, what's redundant/skippable, and a recommended section order.

## A. Multi-page content that collapses into one section each

| Their separate page(s) | Collapses to our single section | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| About / Mission / Philosophy / "Why us" (all sites) | **One "About / Our Approach" section** | TheraPeace and Big Bear each spread this across 6–8 narrative blocks + a values page — compress to 1 short intro + 3–4 value points. |
| Services overview + N individual service pages | **One "Services" section** = card grid + one line each | EmpathyWell (7), Therapy at Play (7), TheraPeace (4), BeHive (4). Individual service pages (each a full templated page) become one card + short blurb; deep content is skippable on a one-pager. |
| Team page + founder page(s) | **One "Meet the Team / Practitioner" section** | Big Bear splits founder + dog across pages; EmpathyWell/BeHive/TheraPeace have a team grid — collapse to one grid (or single bio if solo). |
| "How it works" / Intake / Process / "What happens next" | **One "How It Works / Getting Started" section** (3–4 numbered steps) | EmpathyWell intake, TheraPeace "What Happens Next," Therapy at Play process steps, Big Bear "first appointment" all say the same thing in ~3 steps. |
| FAQ page / FAQ carousel | **One FAQ accordion** | Big Bear's 21 Qs → trim to ~6–8. Keep the recurring universals (see below). |
| Fees / Fees & Insurance page | **One "Fees" block** (or fold into FAQ) | Only EmpathyWell and Big Bear publish numbers; the other five keep fees off-site or in FAQ. Decide whether to publish. |
| Contact page + footer contact | **One "Contact" section + footer** | Every site duplicates contact across a page and the footer; on one page these merge. |
| "What is Play Therapy?" educational block | **One short explainer** (can live inside Services or About) | Center4PlayTherapy devotes a long 6-subheading block to it; on a one-pager, 2–3 sentences + a benefits list is enough. |
| Symptom / "who this is for" checklist | **One "Is this for your child?" section** | Center4PlayTherapy (6 symptoms) and BeHive's "What We Treat" (14 items) — one scannable grid. |

## B. Content that's redundant or skippable when condensing

- **Repeated CTAs between every section** — Center4PlayTherapy repeats "Book a Consultation" ~5×; on one page, keep a hero CTA, one mid-page CTA, and the contact section. Don't repeat after every block.
- **Per-service "Other Services" grids** (Therapy at Play repeats a 6-card grid at the bottom of all 7 service pages) — pure multi-page navigation glue. Gone entirely on a single page; the services section already lists everything.
- **Duplicate contact blocks** — every site states contact on a Contact page *and* in the footer *and* often in a mid-page CTA. Collapse to one contact section + footer.
- **Standalone SEO landing pages** (TheraPeace's two) — these exist only for search ranking and largely restate the services/approach. Not a section; if wanted for SEO, keep as separate hidden URLs, not on the main scroll.
- **Deep service detail** (assessment steps, therapeutic-stage breakdowns, per-modality lists like Big Bear's 7–12 named approaches) — too heavy for a one-pager. Compress each service to a card + one or two lines; move depth to expandable detail only if truly needed.
- **Gallery as its own section** (TheraPeace) — a single image strip within About/Contact is enough; a dedicated gallery page doesn't warrant its own scroll section.
- **Blog / Resources / Library** (Center4PlayTherapy 13 posts, EmpathyWell Resources, Big Bear's 11-category Library) — content hubs that need their own pages. On a single-page site, either omit or reduce to a small "Resources" teaser linking out; don't try to inline a blog.
- **"For Professionals" / "For Schools" / adult-counselling arms** (EmpathyWell, Big Bear) — separate audiences. Skip unless our client actually serves them; they dilute a parent-focused one-pager.
- **Overlapping narrative blocks** — TheraPeace's About runs 8 sequential emotional paragraphs saying one thing ("we understand your child"). On one page, one strong paragraph + a values row replaces the whole sequence.

## C. What consistently earns its place (present across most sites)

These recurred on nearly every reference and should survive the condense:
- A **hero** with a one-line promise + primary CTA (all 7).
- A short **who-we-help / symptoms** cue (Center4PlayTherapy, BeHive, TheraPeace).
- A **services** grid (6 of 7; Root Children has just 2).
- A brief **"what is play therapy / our approach"** explainer (all).
- A **practitioner / team** section with credentials — the core trust signal (all; Root Children leans on accreditation logos instead).
- **How it works / getting started** steps (EmpathyWell, TheraPeace, Therapy at Play, Big Bear).
- **Social proof** — testimonials (Center4, TheraPeace, Therapy at Play, BeHive) or accreditation logos (Root Children, Therapy at Play).
- An **FAQ** (5 of 7).
- A real **contact** section with form + phone + location (all except BeHive, whose Instagram-only stub is the cautionary example).

**Recurring FAQ questions worth keeping** (appeared across multiple sites): "How do I get started?", "Do I stay in the room / come in with my child?", "How will I know if my child needs therapy?", "How long are sessions / how many will we need?", "How much does it cost / do you take insurance?", "Where are you located?"

## D. Suggested single-page section order

Synthesized from the common patterns above — a parent-focused scroll:

1. **Hero** — one-line promise + primary CTA (e.g. "Book a Consultation"). *(all sites lead here)*
2. **Is this for your child? / You're not alone** — short symptom/concern cue grid (Anxiety, Big Feelings, Behavioural, Social, etc.) → reassurance line. *(Center4PlayTherapy checklist + TheraPeace "small worry" framing)*
3. **What is Play Therapy / Our Approach** — 2–3 sentence explainer + 3–4 value points (safe space, child-led, parents involved, whole-child). *(compresses Center4's long block + TheraPeace's 6-item approach + Big Bear's values)*
4. **Services** — card grid, one line each. *(EmpathyWell / Therapy at Play pattern, trimmed)*
5. **How It Works / Getting Started** — 3–4 numbered steps (enquire → consult/intake → sessions → reviews). *(EmpathyWell + TheraPeace + Therapy at Play)*
6. **Meet the Practitioner / Team** — bio(s) + credentials/accreditations (the key trust block). *(all sites)*
7. **Social proof** — testimonials and/or accreditation logos. *(Center4 / TheraPeace / Root Children)*
8. **Fees** *(optional)* — publish a range or fold into FAQ. *(EmpathyWell & Big Bear publish; others don't — a client decision)*
9. **FAQ** — 6–8 of the recurring questions above.
10. **Contact** — form + phone + email + location/hours (+ crisis line if trauma-oriented, per Big Bear). *(avoid BeHive's Instagram-only dead-end)*
11. **Footer** — repeat contact, social, accreditations, land/other acknowledgment if relevant.

*Optional add-ons only if the client wants them (each really wants its own page, not a scroll section): a Blog/Resources teaser linking out, or separate-audience arms (schools/professionals/adult counselling).*
